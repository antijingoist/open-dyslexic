Available applications that include OpenDyslexic

DoxOnBox

DoxOnBox with OpenDyslexic and Beeline viewing Wikipedia

DoxOnBox uses a combination of linguistic tagging, Beelinereader and OpenDyslexic to make content on your iPad more readable. You can pull up articles from Wikipedia or books from your Box account and have them displayed according to your settings. It’s open source also, so you can easily contribute to it or learn from it.

Get it:

  • https://github.com/snarshad/doxonbox (Source)
  • git://github.com/snarshad/doxonbox.git (Clone)

BeeLine Reader

Beeline Reader using the ‘subtle’ option, and OpenDyslexic

Beeline reader is an innovative method to help keep your place while reading. It uses readability to reformat the page, and horizontal gradients across the lines of text. It even has options to use OpenDyslexic. Beeline reader is currently available as bookmarklets. You can add these to your bookmarks bar, and are able to use it across multiple devices easily. Can you tell I use it?

Get it: http://beelinereader.com


Voice Dream Reader

Text to speech app integrated with Instapaper, Paper, dropbox and more. It can display your text in OpenDyslexic so you can read along easily with one of several dozen incredibly high quality voices.

Get it: http://www.voicedream.com


Greek Interlinear Bible

Font selection screen with OpenDyslexic Selected

The Greek Interlinear Bible assists your study of the Original New Testament Greek alongside either the King James/Authorized Version or the Young’s Literal Translation. If you’re serious about studying the original texts, you shouldn’t be without this tool.

Get it: http://photokandy.com/apps/gib/


LiveInk WebClipRead

WebClipRead is an excellent web app that I’ve been using for reading long passages of text. It formats the text in a way similar to what I used to do by hand in college: in small phrases with colors.

Get it: http://liveink.com/WCR


Marvin

Font Selection screen

Get it: http://marvinapp.com


Abilipad

Abilipad is a writing tool with custom keyboards, and more.

Get it: http://appytherapy.com


Freda: the free ebook reader for Windows 8

Freda is an ebook reader with a wealth of options, from fine-grained adjusting of letter smoothing to being able to select fonts like OpenDyslexic

Freda can read EPUB (DRM-free), FB2, HTML and TXT format books. The app understands EPUB formatting information (bold/italic/large text, margins and alignment) and can display images and diagrams in books.

Get it: http://www.turnipsoft.co.uk/freda/windows8/index.htm


Niki Time

Niki Time is a great app that teaches the concept of time and uses OpenDyslexic as the default font.


Niki Time is a clock/calendar for learning.

Niki Time is a clock/calendar learning tool. Parents, teachers and therapists can use this app to help children approaching the time concept: hours, dates, days, months, seasons and weather. The design is simple to increase the usability. While colors have been used to distinguish days, months, seasons, and make the clock easy to read. The clock face is available in two formats: 12 or 24 hours. Seasons beginning is configurable based on the hemisphere where the user lives (northern/boreal or southern/austral).

All Niki accessibility applications use OpenDyslexic as the default typeface.


ER Browser


An excellent web browser that puts you first by letting you select colors and styles, including a variety of typefaces to help you read your pages more easily.

Get it: http://erbrowser.com/
